How are Snowflakes Formed?

A snowflake is either a single ice crystal or an aggregation of ice crystals which falls through the Earth's atmosphere as snow. Each flake nucleates around a dust particle in supersaturated air masses by attracting supercooled cloud water droplets, which freeze and accrete in crystal form.

What Happens in the Spring?

Above 32 degrees Fahrenheit, the snowflakes melt. This Page is intended to help those snowflakes that are currently in meltdown.
